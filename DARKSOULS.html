First Impressions

From your very first moments in Dark Souls, there is magnificence, but also despair. The best and clearest display of this, is in the beginning monologue which reads as such,

("In the Age of Ancients the world was unformed, shrouded by fog. A land of gray crags, Archtrees and Everlasting Dragons. But then there was Fire and with fire came disparity. Heat and cold, life and death, and of course, light and dark. Then from the dark, They came, and found the Souls of Lords within the flame. Nito, the First of the Dead, The Witch of Izalith and her Daughters of Chaos, Gwyn, the Lord of Sunlight, and his faithful knights. And the Furtive Pygmy, so easily forgotten. With the strength of Lords, they challenged the Dragons. Gwyn's mighty bolts peeled apart their stone scales. The Witches weaved great firestorms. Nito unleashed a miasma of death and disease. And Seath the Scaleless betrayed his own, and the Dragons were no more. Thus began the Age of Fire. But soon the flames will fade and only Dark will remain. Even now there are only embers, and man sees not light, but only endless nights. And amongst the living are seen, carriers of the accursed Darksign. Yes, indeed. The Darksign brands the Undead. And in this land, the Undead are corralled and led to the north, where they are locked away, to await the end of the world... This is your fate")

Like the introduction suggests, you awake in the Northern Undead Asylum,(Learn More) abandoned and rotting. The tonal shift from grand god like beings engaged in a mighty war to this desolate waste is indicitive of the themes just introduced, the flame of humanity and light slowly burning away, until only death and dark remains. Your character durring customization appears human, with plump flesh and rosy cheeks, this is where you choose your class, and how you will defend yourself from the horrors of Lordran. All classes start at the same power level, which is increased using the 'souls' of your enemies.

Gifts

Gifts are the last part of character customization, its an object that has remained in your possesion despite your imprisonment in the Asylum, and will in some way aid you in your journey. All have use and merit but diehard fans will tell you which is 'best'. I personally think it to be the Old Witches ring because despite not having much use in the early game, many of the other gifts can be aquired through other means, all except for the Witches Ring. As a big fan of the series as well, the fact that it opens up new dialogue options for certain characters is reason enough.

Orenstien and Smough the duo you saw above, are also one of the most beloved fights in the series! It seems like a bit of an oxymoron, both loved and difficult doesnt seem to add up, but Dark souls players love a challenge, and this fight presents the challenge in such a way that makes for a riveting gameplay experience. The slow cumbersome Smough and the agile pursuer Orenstien, the juxtaposition of these two foes and managing them both at the same time makes for a hard earned victory, and makes it feel that much better when they are eventually defeated.
